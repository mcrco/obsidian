# Problem 2

$$
\begin{pmatrix}
x & 4 & 3 \\
0 & 1 & -1 \\
3 & 5 & y
\end{pmatrix}
 \cdot \begin{pmatrix}
4 & -\frac{13}{3} & -\frac{7}{3} \\
-1 & \frac{5}{3} & \frac{2}{3}   \\
-1 & \frac{2}{3} & \frac{2}{3}
\end{pmatrix}
$$

$$
\begin{align}
4x -4 - 3 &= 1 \\
\implies x &= 2 \\
-7 + \frac{10}{3} + \frac{2}{3}y &= 1 \\
\implies y &= 7
\end{align}
$$
